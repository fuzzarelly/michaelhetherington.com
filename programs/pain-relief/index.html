The Chinese medicine angle

Chinese medicine has a precise understanding of pain: "Where there is free flow, there is no pain. Where there is no free flow, there is pain." Pain is fundamentally a sign of obstruction — of Qi, blood, or both — in a specific area or meridian.

The type of obstruction determines the character of the pain. Cold pain is typically fixed, severe, and relieved by warmth. Heat pain is burning and throbbing. Deficiency pain is dull and aches more with rest. Stagnation pain is distending and changes location.

In this class, Michael uses this TCM framework to help you identify what type of obstruction is most relevant to your pain — so the EFT tapping sequences can be targeted precisely rather than applied generically. This is what makes the approach significantly more effective than standard pain tapping protocols.
