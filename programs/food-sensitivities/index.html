The Chinese medicine angle

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, the Spleen and Stomach govern digestion and the transformation of food into energy. When these systems are weakened — often by chronic stress, irregular eating, cold foods, or emotional suppression — the body's ability to tolerate a wide range of foods decreases.

The Liver also plays a critical role. In TCM, a constrained Liver (often from unresolved frustration, resentment, or emotional tension) "attacks" the digestive system — causing irregular bowel function, bloating, and heightened reactivity to food. This is called Liver Qi Stagnation invading the Spleen.

This class works with all three systems — using EFT to clear the emotional charge stored in these meridians, and giving you the TCM framework to understand why your digestive system is reacting the way it is.
