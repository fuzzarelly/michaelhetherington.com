The Chinese medicine angle

This is where this class goes significantly deeper than standard headache tapping approaches. Chinese medicine classifies headaches into four primary types based on their location, character, and associated organ systems:

  • Excess / Heat headaches — throbbing, frontal or temporal, often associated with Liver Yang Rising. Triggered by stress, frustration, and unresolved anger.
  • Wind-Cold headaches — tight, occipital (back of head), often associated with external cold invasion or poor circulation in the neck.
  • Deficiency headaches — dull, persistent, worsens with fatigue. Associated with Kidney and Blood deficiency — the body simply doesn't have enough fuel to nourish the brain.
  • Sinus / Phlegm headaches — heavy, foggy, frontal. Associated with Spleen dampness and poor digestive function.

Each type requires a different approach. This class gives you a simple method for identifying which type applies to your headaches — and then provides EFT sequences specifically targeted to the relevant organ systems and emotional patterns.

This is why people who have done generic headache tapping with limited results often experience a significant shift when they apply this Chinese medicine framework.
