20 EFT Tapping Affirmations for Healing Your Body

By Michael Hetherington · April 5, 2024

EFT Tapping affirmations are different from regular affirmations — and that difference is what makes them work when ordinary positive self-talk doesn't.

A regular affirmation asks you to simply assert something positive: "I am healthy. I am healed. My body is strong." The problem is that when a part of you doesn't believe that yet, the statement bounces off. The subconscious mind rejects what it experiences as untrue.

EFT affirmations work differently. They acknowledge the current reality first, then pivot toward what's possible. The classic structure is the "setup statement": "Even though [the difficult current reality], I deeply and completely accept myself." This dual acknowledgement — I see what is, and I still accept myself — is what allows the emotional charge to begin releasing.

How to Use These Affirmations

To use these 20 affirmations in your EFT practice:

  1. Tap continuously on the karate chop point (the fleshy outer edge of the hand) as you say each affirmation three times.
  2. Then tap through the standard points — eyebrow, side of eye, under eye, under nose, chin, collarbone, under arm, top of head — repeating a shortened version of the statement at each point.
  3. Pause after each round and check in. Notice any shifts in how your body feels, your emotional state, or the intensity of any symptoms.
  4. Choose the affirmations that feel most resonant or most charged. The more charge you feel when you say it, the more likely it is hitting a real emotional pattern.

You don't need to use all 20 in a single session. Choose 3–5 that feel most relevant and work with those until the emotional charge reduces.

20 EFT Tapping Affirmations for Healing Your Body

  1. "Even though my body has been holding onto pain and tension, I am open to healing and release."
  2. "Even though I feel exhausted and depleted right now, I trust my body knows how to restore itself."
  3. "Even though I have been fighting against what my body is doing, I choose to listen instead."
  4. "Even though this symptom keeps coming back, I believe change is possible and I am open to it."
  5. "Even though I feel frustrated and impatient with my body, I deeply and completely accept myself."
  6. "Even though I have been in pain for so long and I'm tired of it, I choose to release this now."
  7. "Even though my immune system feels weak and depleted, I am worthy of vibrant health."
  8. "Even though I carry tension in my shoulders / chest / gut, I give myself permission to let go."
  9. "Even though I've been told there's nothing more they can do, I believe healing is always possible."
  10. "Even though my digestion has been struggling for so long, I choose ease and flow."
  11. "Even though I'm not sure what's causing this, I trust that my body has the intelligence to heal."
  12. "Even though I've been ignoring what my body is trying to tell me, I am willing to listen now."
  13. "Even though I've been pushing through when I needed to rest, I give myself permission to recover."
  14. "Even though I'm afraid this won't get better, I choose hope and I remain open to change."
  15. "Even though I don't fully understand what's happening in my body, I trust the healing process."
  16. "Even though my energy feels blocked and stuck, I invite flow and vitality to return."
  17. "Even though I've been stressed and my body has absorbed that stress, I am ready to release it."
  18. "Even though I've been disconnected from my body, I choose to reconnect with curiosity and care."
  19. "Even though others have doubted my healing, I know deep healing is possible for me."
  20. "Even though I'm not all the way there yet, I am moving toward greater health and wellbeing with every session."

A Note on Resistance

If any of these affirmations bring up a strong internal reaction — "that's not true," "that's impossible," or a surge of emotion — that's important information. That charge is the very thing EFT is designed to work with.

Stay with that statement. Tap through the karate chop point as you say it. Then tap through the points while acknowledging what came up. The reaction is not resistance to healing — it's the layer that needs to be cleared for healing to deepen.

The more genuinely these statements touch something real in your experience, the more powerful the tapping will be. These are not platitudes to repeat until you believe them — they are doorways into the emotional material that's ready to release.
