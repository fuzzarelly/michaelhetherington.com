Surrogate EFT Tapping: Healing Friends, Family & Animals with EFT Tapping

By Michael Hetherington · March 10, 2024

Surrogate EFT Tapping — sometimes called proxy tapping — is the practice of using yourself as a stand-in to tap on behalf of someone else who is not physically present. It sounds unusual at first. But it has roots in intention-based healing traditions, and many experienced practitioners — including EFT founder Gary Craig, who included surrogate tapping in his original protocols — report consistently positive results.

Whether you attribute the mechanism to quantum field theory, morphogenetic fields, mirror neuron resonance, or simply the power of focused compassionate intention, the practical result is often the same: something shifts — for the person you're tapping for, and sometimes for you as well.

Who Can You Do Surrogate Tapping For?

Surrogate tapping is used for:

  • Friends or family members who are ill, in pain, or emotionally struggling
  • Children who are too young to learn EFT themselves
  • Animals — pets and livestock cannot participate consciously, but respond well to surrogate tapping
  • People who are unwilling or unavailable to receive EFT directly
  • Deceased loved ones, to help heal unresolved grief on both sides
  • Groups of people or communities affected by shared trauma

Most experienced practitioners recommend getting explicit permission where possible — especially when tapping for another adult. If the person cannot consent (a child, an animal, an unconscious patient, or someone who has passed), setting a clear intention that the tapping works "for their highest good" provides an ethical and energetic framework.

How Surrogate Tapping Works

The core principle is intention plus action. You hold a clear intention to connect with and support the other person — then you perform the physical tapping sequence as if you were tapping for their issue. The physical tapping appears to serve as an anchor that grounds and amplifies the intention.

There are two main approaches practitioners use:

Becoming method: You fully embody the other person — imagining yourself as them, tuning into what they are experiencing, and tapping as if you were them. You use first-person language: "Even though I have this pain in my lower back…" as if you were the other person experiencing it.

Holding method: You maintain a clear connection with the other person in your awareness while tapping on yourself in the third person: "Even though [Name] is experiencing this anxiety…" You remain yourself throughout but hold the other person's energy in your field as you tap.

Many practitioners find the becoming method more powerful, but the holding method feels safer and more boundaried — particularly when tapping for someone with intense trauma. Both are valid.

Step-by-Step Guide to Surrogate EFT Tapping

1. Prepare yourself first. Ground your energy. Take a few slow breaths. Clear your own emotional state as much as possible before beginning. If you are anxious, distracted, or emotionally activated, clear that first — otherwise you risk blending your own material with theirs.

2. Set a clear intention. State aloud or internally: "I am tapping on behalf of [Name] with their permission and for their highest good. I am a clear channel for healing and support."

3. Choose your approach. Decide whether to use the becoming method or the holding method. If the other person has very heavy or traumatic material, the holding method gives you more personal protection.

4. Run a standard EFT sequence. Tap through the standard points — karate chop, eyebrow, side of eye, under eye, under nose, chin, collarbone, under arm, top of head — using language that reflects the other person's issue. "Even though [Name] has this [pain / grief / anxiety / fear], I deeply and completely accept [Name]."

5. Notice what arises in you. You may feel emotional during surrogate work. Sensations, sadness, or pressure that doesn't belong to you may move through. This is part of the process. If it becomes overwhelming, return to your own grounded state and take a break before continuing.

6. Close intentionally. At the end of your session, consciously close the energetic connection. A simple statement helps: "I am complete. I release [Name] to their own healing. I return fully to myself." Take a few deep breaths and shake your hands gently to clear any residual energy.

What to Expect

Results with surrogate tapping vary widely. Sometimes the other person reports a shift almost immediately — a reduction in pain, a sense of calm, or a moment of unexpected emotional release. Other times, the effects are subtle and accumulate over multiple sessions. And sometimes, you may not observe anything externally at all — which doesn't mean nothing happened.

Surrogate tapping is best understood as an act of compassionate support — something you can do energetically while the other person also pursues their own healing path. It is not a substitute for medical care, professional mental health support, or the other person's own healing work.

But as an addition to their care, and as a way of channelling your love and attention into something active and purposeful — it is a remarkable and often deeply moving practice.
