EFT Tapping for Headaches and Migraines: A Complete Guide

By Michael Hetherington · September 15, 2024

When a headache or migraine hits, all you want is relief — fast. It's hard to think clearly, work, or enjoy life when there's throbbing pressure in your temples, a heavy ache behind your eyes, or a pounding migraine that makes you want to hide under the covers.

This is where EFT Tapping for headaches and migraines can make a real difference. EFT works directly with the body's stress response to reduce pain, calm the nervous system, and restore balance. And unlike pain medication, it also addresses what's driving the headache — not just the symptom.

First Step: Reduce the Pain

The first priority when you're in pain is simple — reduce the intensity of the headache. EFT is highly effective for this because it helps shift both the physical and emotional tension that fuels pain.

One of the most powerful methods is called "chasing the pain." Here's how it works:

  1. Tune into the pain and give it a rating from 0–10.
  2. Notice where it's located and what it feels like — throbbing, tight, sharp, or dull.
  3. Tap through the EFT points while describing exactly what you feel.
  4. As the pain changes — shifting location, becoming lighter, or less sharp — update your words to match the new sensation.

Often within 10–15 minutes, the intensity drops. Sometimes the headache disappears completely. Even a small reduction is a positive sign that the body is releasing tension and the process is working.

A Simple EFT Script for Headache Relief

Step 1: Tune into your headache. Notice where the pain is, what it feels like, and give it a rating from 0–10.

Setup statement — tap on the karate chop point (side of hand), repeat three times:

  • "Even though I have this headache, and it feels [describe the sensation], I deeply and completely accept myself."
  • "Even though this pain is distracting and overwhelming, I accept myself and how I feel."
  • "Even though I just want this pain to go away, I choose to let my body begin to relax."

Round 1 — Acknowledge the pain (tap through eyebrow, side of eye, under eye, under nose, chin, collarbone, under arm, top of head):

  • "This headache in my [forehead / temples / back of head]."
  • "It feels so [throbbing / heavy / sharp / tight]."
  • "All this tension in my head."
  • "My body is holding onto this pain."
  • "I notice how strong it feels."
  • "It's hard to focus with this headache."
  • "All this pain weighing me down."
  • "I acknowledge it fully right now."

Round 2 — Begin softening the pain:

  • "Allowing this pain to gently soften."
  • "Letting my body know it's safe to relax."
  • "Maybe this pain doesn't need to stay so strong."
  • "I give myself permission to feel calmer."
  • "Letting go of some of this pressure."
  • "Allowing space and flow in my head."
  • "My body knows how to release tension."
  • "Breathing ease into this area now."

Round 3 — Address stress behind the pain:

  • "Some of this pain might be from stress."
  • "My body is carrying so much pressure."
  • "Maybe I've been holding on too tightly."
  • "I allow myself to let go, just a little."
  • "Releasing stress from my shoulders and neck."
  • "Releasing the tightness in my head."
  • "I choose calm over tension."
  • "Letting my nervous system settle down."

Round 4 — Invite relief and balance:

  • "I welcome more ease into my body."
  • "Allowing fresh energy to flow through my head."
  • "Releasing any stuck pressure or emotions."
  • "Inviting balance, calm, and clarity."
  • "My head can relax now."
  • "My mind feels clearer."
  • "My body knows how to heal."
  • "Choosing peace and relief in this moment."

Step 2: Re-check your pain level. Has it shifted, reduced, or changed location? If yes, adjust your words to describe the new sensation and repeat the process.

Step 3: Repeat as needed. Even small changes are a positive sign. Keep tapping until the intensity reduces or you feel calmer and clearer.

Going Deeper: Addressing Root Causes

Once the acute pain has settled, the most important question becomes: why do these headaches keep showing up?

Chronic headaches are rarely random. They tend to signal that something is persistently out of balance — in the nervous system, the emotional landscape, or the energy pathways that Chinese medicine has mapped for thousands of years. EFT can be used to release not only the immediate pain but also the emotions and stress patterns that are contributing to headaches in the first place.

The Chinese Medicine Perspective on Headaches

Traditional Chinese medicine classifies headaches into distinct types based on their location, character, and the organ systems involved. This framework is remarkably useful for identifying which approach to take with EFT — because not all headaches are the same, and not all headaches respond to the same treatment.

The four primary headache types in Chinese medicine are: Liver Yang Rising — throbbing temporal or frontal headaches driven by suppressed frustration, anger, and unresolved stress (the most common type in high-pressure modern living); Wind-Cold invasion — tight, occipital headaches with neck stiffness, often triggered by cold environments or poor circulation in the neck and shoulders; Deficiency headaches — dull, persistent aching that worsens with fatigue, reflecting Blood or Kidney deficiency where the body doesn't have enough energy to nourish the brain; and Sinus or Phlegm-Damp headaches — heavy, foggy, frontal pressure associated with poor digestive function and the accumulation of dampness.

Identifying your headache type shifts EFT from a generic pain-relief tool to a targeted intervention. When you tap with the specific emotional and energetic patterns associated with Liver Yang Rising — suppressed anger, performance pressure, the inability to slow down — the sessions go significantly deeper than simply tapping on "this headache." A Full EFT Tapping Sequence for Headache Relief

Once the acute pain has reduced, this more complete sequence can be used during headache-free periods to address root cause patterns. It's designed for longer, calmer sessions — root cause work rather than acute pain management.

Setup statement (karate chop point):

"Even though I have this pattern of recurring headaches, and I know there's something my body is trying to tell me, I choose to listen and release with kindness."

"Even though stress and tension have been building in my head and neck, I deeply and completely accept myself."

"Even though I've been holding so much — physically and emotionally — I'm open to letting my nervous system finally rest."

Round 1 — The pattern:

  • "My body keeps sending me this signal."

  • "The pressure I put on myself."
  • "All the things I haven't been saying."
  • "All the emotions I've been swallowing."

Round 2 — Release and invite flow:

  • "What if my body could release this now?"

  • "Allowing my Liver Qi to flow without obstruction."
  • "Releasing the frustration I've been storing."
  • "Releasing the pressure I've been creating."
  • "My nervous system is safe to settle."
  • "My head is allowed to be clear, light, and free."

After this round, take a slow breath and notice any change in tension, heaviness, or pressure in your head and neck — even subtle shifts are meaningful.

Why Some Headaches Keep Coming Back

Recurring headaches are a signal, not a disease. They are the body's way of communicating that something is chronically out of balance — and that the underlying condition has not yet been addressed. Pain medication, while sometimes necessary for acute relief, does not change the underlying pattern. It interrupts the signal without resolving what's generating it.

The most common root drivers of recurring headaches are: unresolved emotional tension (particularly suppressed anger, frustration, and unmet needs — all of which stagnate Liver Qi in Chinese medicine); chronic nervous system dysregulation (when the body is stuck in low-grade sympathetic activation, the muscles of the neck, scalp, and jaw remain in a state of perpetual contraction); and accumulated stress patterns that have never been discharged from the body (not just the stress of last week, but the stored emotional charge from months or years of under-processing).

EFT Tapping addresses all three. By stimulating the acupressure points while simultaneously holding the emotional content in awareness, EFT sends a safety signal to the amygdala — the brain's threat-detection centre — while allowing the associated emotional charge to discharge. Over consistent sessions, this reduces both the frequency and intensity of headaches for many people — not by suppressing the signal, but by resolving what's generating it.
