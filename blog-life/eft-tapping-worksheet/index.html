EFT Tapping Worksheet for Manifesting What You Want

By Michael Hetherington · May 20, 2024

Before we get into the worksheet, let's be clear about what "manifesting" actually means in the context of EFT — because it's quite different from simply wishing for something or repeating positive affirmations until you believe them.

In EFT, manifesting is about clearing the emotional blocks that prevent you from receiving what you want. It's not about convincing the universe to give you things. It's about identifying and releasing the internal resistance — the fear, the unworthiness, the "this isn't for people like me" — that keeps what you want at arm's length even when you're working hard for it.

This worksheet gives you a structured process for doing exactly that.

Why EFT Works for Manifesting

When you want something — a healthier body, a thriving business, a deeply loving relationship — there's usually a part of you that wants it. But there's often another part that is actively sabotaging it, or quietly convinced it won't happen, or carrying old beliefs about what you deserve.

These competing beliefs don't cancel each other out neatly. The emotional charge associated with "I'm not good enough" or "this never works out for me" tends to be stronger than the intellectual desire for what you want. The emotionally charged belief usually wins.

EFT changes this by working with the emotional charge directly — not arguing with the belief intellectually, but releasing the energetic grip it has on your nervous system. Once the charge is gone, the belief softens. The internal conflict resolves. And often, what you want becomes significantly easier to move toward.

How to Use the Worksheet

Step 1: Get specific about what you want

Write down what you want to manifest — in specific, grounded terms. Not "I want to be happy" but "I want to feel financially secure, work doing something I love, and wake up without dread." Specificity matters because vague desires don't produce specific resistances to clear.

Step 2: Find the resistance

Now tune into how you feel when you imagine having this thing fully, completely, right now. What comes up? Notice sensations in your body, thoughts, emotions, or a nagging sense of "but..." Write them all down. These are your targets — the specific resistance that EFT will clear.

Common resistance patterns include:

  • "I don't deserve this."
  • "People like me don't get to have this."
  • "If I have this, something bad will happen."
  • "I've wanted this before and it didn't work — why would now be different?"
  • "This feels selfish / greedy / too much to ask."

Step 3: Rate each resistance from 0–10

For each resistance you identified, give it a Subjective Units of Distress (SUDS) rating from 0 (no charge, feels neutral) to 10 (maximum charge, feels very true or very painful). This gives you a baseline to measure against — and lets you prioritise which beliefs carry the most emotional weight.

Step 4: Write your setup statements

For each resistance, write a setup statement using the EFT structure: "Even though [the resistance], I deeply and completely accept myself." For example:

  • "Even though I feel like I don't deserve this, I deeply and completely accept myself."
  • "Even though part of me believes this won't work out, I choose to remain open."
  • "Even though I'm afraid of what will change if I actually get this, I accept how I feel right now."

Step 5: Tap through each setup statement

Take each setup statement and tap on the karate chop point as you repeat it three times. Then tap through the standard points — eyebrow, side of eye, under eye, under nose, chin, collarbone, under arm, top of head — while focusing on the feeling. After each round, take a breath and re-rate the charge from 0–10.

Keep tapping until the charge on each resistance drops to 2 or below. Most resistances require 2–4 rounds of tapping.

Step 6: Pivot to the positive

Once the charge on a resistance has cleared, complete a final round that begins to install the positive version. "I am open to receiving this. I am safe to have this. I am allowed to want this. I am moving toward this with ease." These statements land very differently after the resistance has cleared — because now there's space for them to be true.

Tips for Getting the Most from the Worksheet

  • Do this regularly, not once. New resistances surface as old ones clear. Come back to the worksheet whenever you feel stuck or when your momentum toward a goal stalls.
  • Follow the body, not the logic. The intellectual resistance ("this is unrealistic") often masks a deeper emotional one ("I don't deserve this"). Follow whatever produces the strongest physical sensation.
  • Small shifts count. A resistance moving from 8 to 4 in one session is significant. You don't need to reach zero in one sitting — keep working.
