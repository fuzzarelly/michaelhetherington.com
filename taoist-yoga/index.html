Taoist Yoga

A gentle, internal practice rooted in Taoist philosophy and Chinese medicine — cultivating flow, ease, and connection between body, breath, and energy.

What is Taoist Yoga?

Taoist Yoga draws on the ancient Chinese practice of Yin yoga, Qi Gong, and Taoist philosophy to create a slow, meditative movement practice. Unlike more vigorous or performance-focused yoga styles, Taoist Yoga emphasises relaxation, internal awareness, and the cultivation of Qi (vital energy) through gentle postures and breathwork.

The practice is deeply influenced by the principles of Taoism — the concept of Wu Wei (effortless action), following the natural rhythms of the body and seasons, and the understanding that the body is a microcosm of the natural world.

The benefits of Taoist Yoga

Taoist Yoga works on multiple layers of the human system simultaneously — the physical body, the energetic body, and the mind. Regular practice can support:

  • Deep tissue flexibility, particularly in the joints and connective tissue
  • Improved circulation of blood and Qi through the meridian system
  • Stress reduction and nervous system regulation
  • Emotional processing and release through movement
  • Enhanced body awareness and interoception
  • Balance of the five Chinese medicine organ systems
  • A quieter, more grounded mind

Taoist Yoga and Chinese Medicine

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, the meridians (energy channels) that run through the body are directly influenced by physical postures, breath, and intention. Holding certain positions for extended periods creates a gentle stretch that stimulates the meridians associated with specific organ systems — the liver, gallbladder, kidneys, lungs, and so on.
